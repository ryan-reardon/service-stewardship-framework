# Strategic Stewardship Playbook

## Engineering Manager Guidance

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1. Domain Purpose

Ensure the service remains aligned to business strategy and delivers
measurable value throughout its lifecycle.\Strategic Stewardship is the discipline of continuously validating why the service exists, who it serves, and whether the investment profile
remains justified.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. What Success Looks Like (Level 3--4 Target State)

A strategically mature service demonstrates:

-   A 3--5 year rolling roadmap aligned explicitly to enterprise
    strategic objectives
-   Clearly defined customer segments and validated use cases
-   Quantified business value drivers (revenue enablement, cost
    avoidance, risk reduction, productivity gains)
-   Explicit lifecycle stage (Growth, Scale, Sustain, Sunset)
-   Dedicated modernization capacity (not consumed by reactive work)
-   Annual strategic recalibration embedded into governance
-   Transparent cost-to-value modeling
-   Proactive retirement or consolidation decisions

At Level 4 maturity: - Roadmap investment decisions are informed by
demand forecasting - Scenario modeling (growth, contraction, regulatory
change) influences planning - Adoption and satisfaction trends are
forecasted and acted on early

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Engineering Manager Responsibilities

-   Translate enterprise strategy into actionable engineering priorities
-   Maintain a defensible, capacity-aligned roadmap
-   Ensure every major initiative has a measurable outcome definition
-   Balance reliability, modernization, feature development, and cost
    control
-   Maintain technical debt visibility and remediation planning
-   Continuously reassess service relevance and competitive posture
-   Escalate strategic misalignment early

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Governance Model & Cadence

Weekly: - Review roadmap execution status and risk indicators

Monthly: - Review adoption metrics and financial posture - Validate
capacity allocation across run/grow/modernize work

Quarterly: - Reassess roadmap alignment to enterprise strategy -
Re-evaluate lifecycle stage classification - Review modernization
allocation effectiveness

Annually: - Conduct structured lifecycle review - Challenge continued
relevance of service - Approve major investment or sunset decisions

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Required Artifacts

-   3--5 year rolling roadmap (12-quarter view minimum)
-   Documented value realization model
-   Customer and use-case inventory
-   Lifecycle classification statement
-   Technical debt register
-   Capacity allocation model (run vs grow vs modernize)
-   Strategic risk log
-   Financial posture summary (TCO and cost-to-value trend)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. Operating Mechanisms

-   Roadmap scoring rubric tied to measurable business outcomes
-   Initiative intake governance process
-   Explicit modernization capacity guardrail (e.g., minimum allocation
    threshold)
-   Sunset trigger policy for low-value services
-   Quarterly strategic narrative published to stakeholders
-   Capacity alignment checkpoint before accepting new commitments

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7. Common Failure Modes (Anti-Patterns)

-   Roadmap driven by loudest stakeholder
-   No quantified outcomes tied to initiatives
-   Modernization perpetually deferred
-   Services never retired
-   Capacity overcommitment without trade-off clarity
-   Strategic discussions replaced by reactive firefighting
-   Financial posture reviewed only during budget season

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. Maturity Advancement Roadmap

Level 1 → Reactive: - Identify named service owner - Create documented
12-month roadmap - Document primary customers and use cases

Level 2 → Managed: - Quantify initiative-level business outcomes -
Introduce lifecycle stage classification - Track adoption and
satisfaction trends - Establish modernization allocation rule

Level 3 → Optimized: - Implement scenario-based investment modeling -
Formalize capacity allocation model - Publish quarterly strategic review
summary - Track cost-to-value ratio trends

Level 4 → Predictive & Autonomous: - Integrate demand forecasting into
roadmap planning - Automate strategic metric dashboards - Establish
early-warning indicators for lifecycle transitions

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 9. Monthly Metrics Dashboard

-   Adoption growth rate
-   Customer satisfaction trend
-   Roadmap execution % complete
-   Capacity allocation (run/grow/modernize)
-   Cost-to-value ratio
-   Technical debt trend
-   Forecast vs actual delivery variance
-   Investment mix distribution

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 10. 90-Day Action Plan for a New Manager

Days 1--30: 
- Identify and map all stakeholders (business, security,
operations, finance, upstream/downstream teams) 
- Audit the existing roadmap for clarity, scope realism, and alignment to enterprise
priorities 
- Audit financial posture including TCO, major cost drivers, and budget variance 
- Review lifecycle stage classification (if none exists, draft one) 
- Review technical debt register and modernization backlog 
- Review adoption and satisfaction data 
- Assess capacity allocation across run/grow/modernize categories

Days 31--60: 
- Conduct stakeholder interviews to validate use cases and
value assumptions 
- Quantify business outcomes for top roadmap initiatives 
- Establish formal roadmap review cadence 
- Introduce modernization allocation guardrail if absent 
- Draft updated lifecycle stage statement 
- Identify initiatives misaligned to strategy and recommend adjustment 
- Establish a strategic metrics dashboard

Days 61--90: 
- Publish updated 3--5 year rolling roadmap 
- Communicate strategic narrative and trade-offs to stakeholders 
- Implement capacity alignment model 
- Formalize modernization and debt reduction targets 
- Establish quarterly strategic review forum 
- Define explicit retirement or consolidation candidates (if applicable) 
- Document and socialize service investment thesis
