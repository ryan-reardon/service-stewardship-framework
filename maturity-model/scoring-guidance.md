# Scoring Guidance

This document defines how to score domains within the Service Stewardship Framework maturity model.

The purpose of scoring is clarity.

It is not certification.
It is not compliance.
It is not reputation management.

It is structural diagnosis.

---

# Scoring Principles

All maturity scoring must follow five principles:

1. Evidence over opinion  
2. Conservative classification  
3. Domain independence  
4. Governance validation  
5. Periodic reassessment  

If a maturity claim cannot be supported with artifacts, metrics, and demonstrated governance, it does not qualify.

---

# Evidence Requirements

A domain may only be classified at a given level if all of the following are true:

- Required artifacts exist and are current.
- Metrics are instrumented and reviewed regularly.
- Governance cadence is active.
- Ownership is clearly defined.
- Improvements are measurable and tracked.

If any of these conditions are missing, score the lower level.

---

# How to Determine Domain Level

Each domain should be evaluated independently.

Use the following decision model:

### Step 1: Identify the Lowest Unsatisfied Requirement

Review the expectations of each maturity level sequentially.

If a Level 3 expectation is not met, the domain cannot be Level 3.

### Step 2: Validate with Evidence

For each claim:

- Request documentation.
- Review dashboards.
- Confirm governance cadence.
- Validate stakeholder awareness.

If evidence is incomplete, downgrade.

### Step 3: Confirm Sustainability

A process that exists but depends on one individual does not qualify as Level 3 or Level 4.

Maturity requires systemic durability.

---

# Conservative Scoring Rule

When uncertain between two levels, choose the lower level. This model exists to surface risk, not conceal it. Inflated scoring prevents improvement.

---

# Cross-Domain Risk Consideration

Do not average domain scores mechanically.

Certain domains carry disproportionate enterprise risk:

- Security & Risk Governance
- Operational Reliability
- Financial Stewardship
- Architectural Integrity

A single Level 1 rating in these domains may warrant immediate prioritization regardless of overall maturity.

---

# Service-Level Maturity Interpretation

Service maturity is directional, not arithmetic.

Avoid calculating a simple mean score.

Instead:

- Identify lowest domains.
- Identify highest-risk domains.
- Identify misaligned domains (e.g., high delivery, low reliability).

Maturity imbalance is often more dangerous than uniformly low maturity.

---

# Governance Cadence

Scoring should occur:

- At initial adoption (baseline)
- Quarterly for priority domains
- Annually for full reassessment

Scoring sessions should include:

- Service owner
- Technical lead
- Cross-functional stakeholder (optional but recommended)

Document:

- Score assigned
- Evidence reviewed
- Identified gaps
- Target maturity level
- Review date

---

# Maturity Progression Criteria

To move from one level to the next, the following must be true:

- Structural gaps are resolved.
- Governance cadence is operating consistently.
- Metrics show sustained improvement.
- Risk exposure has measurably decreased.
- Ownership is durable and not person-dependent.

Progression should be intentional, not symbolic.

---

# Common Scoring Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

• Aspirational scoring  
• “We plan to implement” justification  
• Tool-based scoring without governance  
• Scoring based on effort rather than outcome  
• Scoring without stakeholder review  

If maturity claims do not change decision-making behavior, they are not real.

---

# Documentation of Scores

For each domain, document:

- Current level
- Evidence summary
- Identified gaps
- Target level
- Planned improvements
- Target review date

Maintain scoring history to track progression over time.

Trend is as important as current state.

---

# The Objective

The maturity model is a structural lens.

It reveals:

- Where risk accumulates
- Where governance is weak
- Where modernization is overdue
- Where ownership is fragile

It is a leadership instrument. It exists to strengthen stewardship, not to certify it.
