# Strategic Stewardship Assessment

This assessment evaluates the maturity of Strategic Stewardship within a technology service.

The objective is structural clarity, not aspirational scoring.

Score conservatively.  
Require evidence.  
Default to the lower level when uncertain.

---

# Domain Purpose

Strategic Stewardship ensures the service exists for clear, validated reasons, delivers measurable value, and evolves intentionally across its lifecycle.

---

# Level 1: Reactive

Strategic direction is informal or implied.

Characteristics:

- No documented multi-year roadmap
- Service purpose not formally articulated
- No defined lifecycle classification
- Business outcomes not quantified
- Modernization occurs only after crisis
- Investment decisions are reactive

Evidence Review:

- Is there a written roadmap?
- Is there documented lifecycle stage?
- Are business outcomes quantified?
- Is modernization capacity explicitly allocated?

If most answers are “no,” score Level 1.

---

# Level 2: Managed

Basic strategic clarity exists, but governance is limited.

Characteristics:

- Documented roadmap (1–3 years)
- Service purpose articulated
- Lifecycle stage defined
- Annual strategic review occurs
- Some quantification of business value
- Technical debt tracked but not actively governed

Evidence Review:

- Roadmap document exists and is current
- Lifecycle stage documented
- Annual review calendarized
- Outcome metrics identified
- Debt register exists

If these are present but not deeply integrated into investment decisions, score Level 2.

---

# Level 3: Optimized

Strategic direction actively drives investment and modernization.

Characteristics:

- 3–5 year roadmap aligned to enterprise strategy
- Explicit investment allocation (run / grow / modernize)
- Quantified value tracking (cost-to-value, adoption, ROI)
- Lifecycle stage influences funding decisions
- Modernization is planned, not reactive
- Trade-offs are documented and reviewed

Evidence Review:

- Roadmap alignment to enterprise objectives documented
- Budget allocation mapped to lifecycle stage
- Cost-to-value metrics tracked
- Modernization backlog prioritized intentionally
- Strategic trade-offs documented

If strategic clarity measurably influences funding and execution, score Level 3.

---

# Level 4: Proactive

Strategic posture is predictive and continuously refined.

Characteristics:

- Strategy informed by leading indicators (telemetry, adoption trends, cost curves)
- Lifecycle transitions managed intentionally (scale → optimize → sunset)
- Investment portfolio dynamically adjusted
- Modernization preempts risk accumulation
- Strategic risks modeled and monitored
- Cross-service portfolio alignment actively governed

Evidence Review:

- Predictive metrics inform roadmap changes
- Lifecycle transition criteria defined
- Scenario modeling performed (growth, decline, stress)
- Enterprise portfolio dependencies reviewed regularly
- Modernization reduces measurable risk before incident

If strategic decisions are anticipatory rather than reactive, score Level 4.

---

# Scoring Summary

| Level | Posture |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Reactive and informal |
| 2 | Documented but basic |
| 3 | Aligned and investment-driven |
| 4 | Predictive and enterprise-integrated |

---

# Risk Considerations

Strategic Stewardship weaknesses often manifest as:

- Chronic modernization backlog
- Escalating technical debt
- Misaligned investment
- Cost growth without value growth
- Architectural stagnation
- Surprise lifecycle collapse

Low maturity in this domain creates long-term systemic risk even if operational reliability appears strong.

---

# Target Level & Improvement Plan

After scoring:

1. Document current level.
2. Define target level (typically +1).
3. Identify structural gaps.
4. Assign accountable owner.
5. Define timeline for reassessment.

Reassess quarterly for active improvement periods.

---

# Final Reminder

A service can appear stable operationally while failing strategically.
Strategic Stewardship determines whether the service endures.
Score honestly.
