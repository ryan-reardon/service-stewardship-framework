# Organizational Capability Assessment

This assessment evaluates the maturity of Organizational Capability within a technology service.

The objective is structural durability, not optimistic scoring.

Score conservatively.  
Require evidence.  
Default to the lower level when uncertain.

---

# Domain Purpose

Organizational Capability ensures that ownership, skills, and team structure are intentionally designed to sustain, operate, and evolve the service over time.

Architecture alone does not create resilience.  
Institutional capability does.

---

# Level 1: Reactive

Ownership and capability are informal or person-dependent.

Characteristics:

- No clearly documented service owner
- Decision rights ambiguous or situational
- Operational responsibilities distributed by assumption
- Critical skills concentrated in individuals
- Knowledge undocumented or tribal
- Team structure shifts frequently

Evidence Review:

- Is service ownership formally documented?
- Are decision rights defined?
- Is knowledge institutionalized?
- Are skills redundantly distributed?
- Is capacity explicitly allocated?

If most answers are “no,” score Level 1.

---

# Level 2: Managed

Ownership exists but structural rigor is limited.

Characteristics:

- Named service owner
- Basic role clarity defined
- Core operational skills present
- Documentation exists but unevenly maintained
- Capacity planning informal
- Modernization competes with reactive load

Evidence Review:

- Service owner documented
- Basic RACI or responsibility model exists
- Skills inventory partially defined
- Team charter documented
- Some capacity tracking present

If structure exists but does not consistently influence execution stability, score Level 2.

---

# Level 3: Optimized

Ownership and capability are intentionally structured and lifecycle-aware.

Characteristics:

- Explicit, documented service ownership
- Decision rights defined and durable
- Skill composition aligned to operational and strategic goals
- Planned capacity allocation (run / improve / modernize)
- Knowledge embedded in documentation and shared practices
- Team structure stable relative to lifecycle stage

Evidence Review:

- Ownership model documented and reviewed
- Capacity allocation explicitly tracked
- Skill gaps identified and addressed
- Cross-training or redundancy implemented
- Structure aligned to lifecycle classification

If capability structure measurably improves execution predictability and resilience, score Level 3.

---

# Level 4: Proactive

Organizational capability is continuously evaluated and intentionally evolved.

Characteristics:

- Ownership model reviewed on governance cadence
- Capability gaps identified before constraint
- Succession planning documented
- Capacity adjusted as lifecycle shifts
- Structure resilient to growth, stress, or attrition
- Institutional knowledge independent of individuals

Evidence Review:

- Governance cadence includes capability review
- Succession or redundancy planning documented
- Capacity reallocation driven by lifecycle changes
- Structural risks actively tracked
- Capability forecasting performed

If capability decisions are anticipatory rather than reactive, score Level 4.

---

# Scoring Summary

| Level | Posture |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Person-dependent and informal |
| 2 | Defined but fragile |
| 3 | Structured and lifecycle-aligned |
| 4 | Anticipatory and resilient |

---

# Risk Considerations

Organizational Capability weaknesses often manifest as:

- Incident escalation delays
- Dependency on key individuals
- Modernization stagnation
- Capacity saturation
- Loss of continuity during attrition
- Initiative failure due to skill gaps

Low maturity in this domain suppresses progress in Operational Reliability, Engineering Excellence, and Innovation.

Structural fragility compounds under stress.

---

# Target Level & Improvement Plan

After scoring:

1. Document current level.
2. Define target level (aligned to lifecycle and criticality).
3. Identify structural capability gaps.
4. Assign accountable owner.
5. Define timeline for reassessment.

Reassess quarterly during active structural adjustment.

---

# Final Reminder

A service can appear technically sound while being organizationally fragile. Organizational Capability determines whether service ownership endures beyond individuals. Score honestly.
